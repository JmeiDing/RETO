 1. Hackers sent spearphishing emails to targets in 2014 impersonating IT systems of healthcare companies. 2. If emails were opened, malware like Derusbi/Sakula would infect the systems and communicate with command servers controlled by the hackers. 3. The malware gathered data from the systems over months. 4. Eventually the hackers were able to steal personal data on tens of millions of customers from Anthem's systems. 5. The data was likely used for Chinese cyber espionage purposes rather than financial theft.
