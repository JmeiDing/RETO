1. In Spring 2022, the company discovered one of its devices communicating with a known C2 server. 2. Investigation found that APT27 had compromised the network for over a year. 3. On March 4, 2021, APT27 exploited ProxyLogon vulnerabilities in an Exchange server for initial access and remote code execution. 4. Over 9 months, APT27 moved laterally to compromise 5 different domains in the network. 5. They used reconnaissance like PowerShell scripts to map hosts, users, and policies. 6. They stole credentials with tools like Mimikatz. 7. In month 11, APT27 began exfiltrating large volumes of data - nearly 3 TB over 17 days. 8. The exfiltrated data was business documents, personal info, and technical data. 9. The data was archived locally first. 10. Then it was staged and exfiltrated out through the compromised Exchange server. 11. APT27 used techniques to hide their activity like disabling security tools, deleting files, masquerading tasks, and using proxy tools.
