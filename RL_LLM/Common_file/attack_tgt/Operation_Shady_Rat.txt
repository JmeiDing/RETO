 1. The hackers conducted spear phishing attacks containing links to malware. 2. Victims who clicked the links unwittingly loaded remote access trojan (RAT) malware onto their computers. 3. The RAT malware opened up remote access to allow the hackers to get into the victims' networks. 4. The hackers escalated privileges to gain user-level access and begin exfiltrating data from the victims' systems. 5. Sensitive data including government secrets, emails, legal documents, and design blueprints were stolen over a period of years. 6. The command and control server used to manage the RAT malware helped the hackers continue stealing data from victims.
