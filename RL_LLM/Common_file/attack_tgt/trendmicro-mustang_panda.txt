 1. Spear-phishing emails are sent containing links to archives on Google Drive. The emails use fake accounts but may spoof real internal contacts. 2. The archives contain executables like TONEINS, TONESHELL, and PUBLOAD. 3. Users are lured into downloading and executing the malware. TONEINS installs TONESHELL. 4. TONESHELL establishes persistence using schtasks or registry run keys. 5. TONESHELL phones home to C2 servers and can download additional modules. 6. The malware exfiltrates data and allows remote control of the compromised system.
