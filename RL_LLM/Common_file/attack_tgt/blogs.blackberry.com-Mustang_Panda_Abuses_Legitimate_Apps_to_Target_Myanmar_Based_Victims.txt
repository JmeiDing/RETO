1. Mustang Panda sends phishing emails with malicious document attachments. 2. If the attachment is opened, it deploys the initial malware payload. 3. The payload communicates with domains masquerading as Myanmar news outlets. 4. The payload downloads RAR files containing legitimate signed HP utilities. 5. The RAR also contains a DLL loader and encrypted PlugX payload files. 6. Upon execution, the legitimate app loads the malicious DLLs. 7. The DLLs decrypt and deploy the PlugX payload. 8. PlugX allows the threat actor remote access to compromise the victim system.
