1. The malware infects Android devices through drive-by downloads or malicious apps. 2. It exploits vulnerabilities to gain root access on the device. 3. If rooting fails, it uses fake system updates to trick users into granting permissions. 4. It downloads and installs fraudulent apps on the infected devices. 5. The apps display fake ads, create fake clicks/impressions, and install more fraudulent apps. 6. The command and control platform manages the infected devices and tracks fraudulent activities. 7. The ad revenue and traffic data is monitored through the C&C dashboard. 8. Data and access to infected devices could be sold to other cybercriminals.
