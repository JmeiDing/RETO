1. Threat actors sign up as customers of CloudEyE. 2. They use the CloudEyE builder to encrypt their malware. 3. The CloudEyE builder generates an encrypted dropper for the malware. 4. The threat actor uploads the encrypted malware to Google Drive or another cloud drive. 5. The encrypted dropper is distributed to victims. 6. When run, the dropper fetches the encrypted payload from the cloud drive. 7. The payload is decrypted and executed on the victim's machine. 8. The encryption and code randomization defeats AV detection. 9. The threat actor gains access to the infected machines.
