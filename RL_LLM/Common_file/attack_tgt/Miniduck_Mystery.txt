1. The attacker sends a spearphishing email with a malicious PDF attachment exploiting CVE-2013-0640. 2. The victim opens the PDF, triggering the exploit. 3. The exploit installs the first stage MiniDuke backdoor DLL. 4. The backdoor sets persistence via a startup LNK file and contacts the C2 over Twitter. 5. The C2 responds with encrypted fake GIFs containing second stage backdoors. 6. The second stage backdoors can download additional modules and exfiltrate documents. 7. Larger third stage backdoors are sent to specific victims for enhanced capabilities. 8. The backdoors fingerprint the infected system and send info to the command servers.
