1. The attacker sends a phishing email with an Excel document containing malicious macros. 2. The macros fetch scripts that collect system information and download a trojanized TeamViewer DLL. 3. The DLL stealthily launches TeamViewer and steals session credentials. 4. The attacker remotely connects via TeamViewer to control the compromised machine. 5. The DLL can also download additional payloads for further malware functionality.
