1. The threat actors register doppelganger domains mimicking legitimate organizations, using TLDs that provide anonymity. 2. The actors create fake websites by copying content from real websites of targeted organizations. 3. Phishing emails are sent to victims directing them to the fake websites. 4. Victims visit the fake websites, believing them to be legitimate, and enter their login credentials. 5. The threat actors harvest the victim credentials entered on the fake sites. 6. The credentials can then be used by the actors to gain unauthorized access to the real websites and accounts.
