APT37 (Advanced Persistent Threat 37) is a sophisticated and advanced North Korean cyber espionage group that has been targeting various entities, including Middle Eastern companies with business ties to North Korea. APT37 uses spear phishing, strategic web compromises, and torrent sites to distribute malware. Lures often relate to Korea. The group exploits vulnerabilities in software like Hangul Word Processor and Adobe Flash. They have access to zero-days. APT37 has also been leveraging compromised servers, messaging platforms, and cloud service providers for command and control. The group's activities have evolved over time to evade detection by network defenders. In 2017, APT37 targeted a Middle Eastern company involved in international affairs and trade issues, as well as individuals working with human rights organizations. The group has also been known to target South Korean institutions conducting research on North Korea.
