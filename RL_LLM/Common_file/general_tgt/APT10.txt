The report analyzes a cyberespionage campaign targeting a Norwegian MSP and US companies by Chinese APT group APT10. The attackers gained initial access through Citrix and LogMeIn remote access software using stolen credentials. They then conducted privilege escalation using DLL sideloading to deploy malware including a Trochilus variant and UPPERCUT backdoor. APT10 used BITSAdmin for C2 transfer and task scheduling, and Mimikatz for credentials theft. Proprietary data was exfiltrated compressed with WinRAR and uploaded with cURL to a Dropbox account. The campaign is assessed to be APT10 conducting espionage on behalf of China's Ministry of State Security based on TTPs.
